Life Homes

What are Life Homes?

The Life Home Village is a place where abused, neglected, and abandoned children are given the loving care every child needs — and a place where they thrive.

When our Sponsorship Program began in 2006, we quickly saw children's lives improve through daily food, clean water, basic health care, and education. But our partners at Bombo Pentecostal Church recognized that some children weren't progressing at the same rate as others. Their home environments were unstable, and many endured physical, mental, or emotional abuse.

In response, BPC and Align worked with sponsors to build homes where these children could flourish. Our first Life Home was completed in 2012, followed by the second in 2013. The transformation we witnessed in these children was so profound that we began envisioning a full Life Home community — the Village that stands today.

Life at the Village

A refuge where children are cared for

Housing up to 40 children across four homes, the Life Home Village is a place of physical, emotional, and spiritual care — and a foundation for the rest of a child's life.

Daily Life

A loving family environment

Each of the four homes is led by a designated caretaker dedicated to creating a stable, loving family for the children who live there — so every child can grow up feeling seen, safe, and known.

Life Skills

Equipped for what comes next

Children are given daily responsibilities that teach vital life skills and the value of working together — habits they will carry into adulthood and their future spheres of influence.

Looking Ahead

Growing into a community

Thanks to years of faithful donors, the Village has grown to include a playground, sports equipment, a community center, and dedicated spaces for learning, growth, and play — a safe haven for the children here today and those yet to come.

A Life Transformed

Godfrey Ssekito

From searching for food to building a future

Godfrey was brought to the attention of our BPC partners in 2014. As the eldest boy of a child-led home of four, he spent his days searching for food and other essentials to help his siblings survive.

That same year, God blessed the entire family when all of them were sponsored through our program. Soon after, all four children were enrolled in school and moved into Life Homes — where God continued to build Godfrey up as a young man of character.

Today, with the help of his sponsors and our generous Scholarship Program supporters, Godfrey is in his final year at Gombe Community Polytechnic School, where he is studying toward a certificate in Motor Vehicle Mechanics.

We have already witnessed Godfrey's incredible commitment to care for his siblings, and we look forward to seeing how God uses him to make an impact in his community.
